Importance of Trees

Trees are vital for life on Earth. They provide oxygen, improve air quality, conserve water, preserve soil, and support wildlife.

They also provide shade, reduce noise pollution, and can be used as building materials, fuel, and food.

Types of Trees

  • Deciduous Trees: Trees that shed their leaves annually. Examples include oak, maple, and birch.
  • Evergreen Trees: Trees that retain their leaves throughout the year. Examples include pine, spruce, and fir.
  • Fruit Trees: Trees that produce fruit. Examples include apple, cherry, and orange trees.

Interesting Facts

  • Trees can live for thousands of years. The oldest known tree is a bristlecone pine named Methuselah, which is over 4,800 years old.
  • The largest tree by volume is the General Sherman, a giant sequoia in California's Sequoia National Park.
  • Trees communicate with each other through underground networks of fungi, often referred to as the "Wood Wide Web."
